# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fellow engineers and peers reading deeply. They judge technical taste, problem-solving approach, and craftsmanship signals. They notice details — a sloppy interaction or generic layout undermines credibility faster than a missing skill badge.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio for a backend developer that communicates taste through its own design. The site itself is the primary proof of craft. It must feel bold, kinetic, and intentional — not a template, not corporate, not safe.

## Brand Personality

Bold, playful, kinetic. Engineering confidence expressed through motion, color commitment, and spatial precision. Like Vercel's geometric energy and Stripe's smooth reveals, but with its own orange identity.

## Anti-references

- Overly corporate: navy suits, stock photography, sterile layouts, safe color, zero personality
- Generic dev portfolio templates: photo + bio + skill bars + project cards in a centered column
- Anything that reads as "AI generated a SaaS landing page"

## Design Principles

1. **Taste over claims.** The site's own design proves the point. No "I care about UX" — show it through execution.
2. **Motion with intent.** Every transition earns its place. Kinetic energy that guides, not distracts.
3. **Engineer to engineer.** No marketing fluff. Peer-to-peer voice. Details that engineers notice.
4. **Commit to the color.** Orange is the identity. No hedging with safe neutrals around the edges.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort. Reasonable contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader support. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` but no formal WCAG target.
